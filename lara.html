Lära

A current work-in-progress in designing and building a desk lamp using bent lamination.

The name comes from the Swedish verb lära sig which means to learn or to study.

I was curious about contemporary woodworking techniques and wanted to try my hand at bent laminate after learning about it. The process seemed simple enough; by pressing glued thin slats of wood by into a form, the wood can take on new forms without compromising strength and structure.

When I think of bent lamination, I am immediately drawn to the POÄNG armchair by IKEA - a staple of my childhood.

In fact, my parents still own a pair of POÄNGs that they built together when they met.

I knew I wanted to pursue a unibody construction that looked clean, elegant and inspired by Scandinavian design.

Testing the Circuitry

❮ ❯

After acquiring all the electronic components, I opened up the rocker switch to check if there was sufficient circuit protection.

The rocker switch has no built-in protection, so I will be soldering a simple decoupling capacitor into the lamp during final construction.

Next, I set up a 24-hour test run to determine expected temperatures and/or if I need to make some changes to deal with the heat output. My XiaoYi camera will take screenshots whenever there's a change on my multimeter.

Construction

I had some particle board lying around and used it to build my form. The form will be lined with some sheet cork to protect the wood slats and help distribute even pressure when forming the laminate.

The body will be constructed in two parts - an outer and inner half. A slot has to be routed out from the outer half to run the wiring up the body. The inner half will then be glued to enclose the wiring.

The recessed cavity in where the LED strip sits will also be routed out. The outer half will be routed with a 3/4-inch straight bit. The inner half will have a similar slot routed with a 1/2-inch width. When the two halves are glued together, a ledge will be created for the plastic diffuser to snap into.

The diffuser for the lamp be 3-D printed, likely sanded and epoxied for a sand-blasted finish.

Building Prep

❮ ❯

My local lumber mill has some great rift sawn walnut and ash. Before I can rip the lumber into thin slats, I needed to make a zero clearance insert for my table saw. This will prevent any tearout when ripping wood slats for lamination.

Currently, I am working on the bending form - I saved both portions in case I want a two-part bending form.

When forming the laminate, the glued slats will be separated by a plastic sheet to form the inner and outer half for construction.

That's it, folks! Stay tuned for more!
